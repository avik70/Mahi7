Exhaustive events:

In probability, exhaustive is a condition of two or more events which serves a great role in finding the probability as it changes if the events are exhaustive or not. Two or more events are said to be exhaustive if there is a certain chance of occurrence of at least one of them when they are all considered together.

consider the experiment of a fair die being thrown. Then there are six outcomes and all of them are equally likely to occur.

events of getting different numbers taken together are exhaustive as together at least one of them is certain to happen.

For if we take events like getting a odd number and a number that prime will not be certain to occur and hence are not exhaustive.
